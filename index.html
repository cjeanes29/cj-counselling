A bit about me

Hi, I’m Charlotte, a fully qualified, Integrative Counsellor offering an open-minded, compassionate space where you can truly be yourself and explore your thoughts, feelings, and emotions in a judgment-free environment.
I believe that everyone experiences and processes the world differently, so I tailor my approach to suit your individual needs and what makes you feel most comfortable. Starting therapy can feel daunting, but you don’t have to face it alone. I’ll walk alongside you at your pace, creating a space where you feel safe, heard, and supported.
I work in a person-centred approach, incorporating elements from other modalities such as psychodynamic, Gestalt, CBT and transactional analysis.
This integrative approach allows me to draw on a variety of techniques, including creative interventions. Whether you enjoy art, writing, or other expressive tools, we can explore what feels most natural and helpful for you.
I work with adults from all walks of life. Whether you're feeling stuck, struggling with anxiety or depression, or want to explore past experiences in a safe and empowering environment, you're welcome here.

I aim to offer warmth, patience, and a space where you can feel free to explore your thoughts and experiences without fear of judgment—somewhere you can show up as your full, authentic self and be met with acceptance. If this sounds like the kind of support you’re looking for, please feel free to get in touch. I’d love to talk about how we can begin your journey together. I’m a newly qualified counsellor with a Foundation Degree in Integrative Counselling and am a registered member of the National Counselling and Psychotherapy Society (NCPS). As part of my training, I worked for over a year as a volunteer counsellor with a low-cost counselling service, gaining valuable experience supporting a diverse range of clients. My core approach is person-centred, while also drawing on elements from psychodynamic therapy, Gestalt, transactional analysis, and CBT. This integrative way of working allows me to tailor sessions to suit each client’s individual needs.

Fees & Payment

My current fee is £50 per 50-minute session in person and £45 per 50-minute session online.
I believe counselling should be accessible to as many people as possible, so I offer a limited number of low-cost slots, as well as concessions for students and those working in the veterinary industry. Please feel free to ask about availability.
Payment is requested by bank transfer before each appointment. Failure to provide payment in advance may result in the session being cancelled.

Cancellations & Complaints

Cancellation Policy

Life can be unpredictable, and sometimes you may need to cancel a session. I kindly ask for at least 24 hours' notice—cancellations made with less than 24 hours' notice will be charged at the full session fee.
If, for any reason, I need to cancel a session, I will give you as much notice as possible and do my best to rearrange at a time that suits you. Of course, there will be no charge in these instances.

What if I have a complaint?

I aim to provide a safe, respectful, and professional counselling service. However, if you ever feel unhappy with any aspect of our work together, I encourage you to raise it with me so we can address it sensitively and constructively. If you feel unable to resolve the issue directly, you have the right to make a formal complaint to my professional body, the National Counselling and Psychotherapy Society (NCPS). You can find more information about their complaints process at https://ncps.com/complaints/complaints-process.

Timings

Each counselling session lasts 50 minutes. If you arrive late, the session will still end at the scheduled time, as I’m unable to extend sessions beyond the allotted slot. This helps ensure fairness and consistency for all clients.

How often would we meet?

Sessions are typically held on a weekly basis, but this can vary depending on your individual needs and circumstances. We can talk about what might work best for you during a free 20-minute initial consultation.

Reasons to seek Counselling

Counselling really is for everyone. There are many reasons someone may choose to seek support. You might be navigating a life change, processing a recent or past event, or feeling stuck and unsure of how to move forward.
You don’t need to be in crisis to benefit from counselling—sometimes it’s simply about having a safe space to talk, reflect, and be heard without judgement. Whatever your reason, counselling can support you in gaining clarity, building resilience, and understanding yourself more deeply.

What is Counselling?

Counselling is a type of talking therapy that takes place with a qualified counsellor in a safe, confidential, and non-judgemental space. It offers the opportunity to explore your thoughts, feelings, and behaviours, with the support of someone trained to listen and help you make sense of what you’re experiencing. Counselling can support you in managing emotional distress, developing healthier coping strategies, and gaining a deeper understanding of yourself and how you respond to life’s challenges.

Will anyone find out?

Our sessions are fully confidential. Whether or not you choose to tell others that you're having counselling is entirely up to you. Some people find it helpful to have support from friends or family, while others prefer to keep it private. Whatever you decide, it's about what feels right and comfortable for you.
